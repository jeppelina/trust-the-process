Stick Figures v2 — More Life

Same simplicity, more personality. The idea: vary the body shapes, give faces real expressions, let posture do the acting. Every character should be recognizable from across the room as a silhouette.

Steve — The Accountant

Stiff, upright, slightly hunched from desk work. Everything about him is straight lines and right angles. He doesn't belong here and his body knows it. Narrow shoulders, rigid posture, death grip on his briefcase.

Neutral — tense, gripping briefcase
Confused — head tilt, scratching
Frustrated — fists, dropped briefcase
Scared — briefcase shield, backing up

Essential Oil Warrior

Everything about her is curved and flowing where Steve is rigid. She floats rather than walks. Arms always doing something expressive — holding a bottle up, gesturing grandly, blessing you. Wide hips, flowing skirt, head slightly tilted back (perpetually looking down her nose at you). Serene smile that never breaks.

Default — serene, blessing pose
Attack — hurling oils, still smiling

Breathwork Monk

Wide, immovable, rooted. Where Steve is all verticals, the Monk is a triangle — broad base, narrow top. No mouth (communicates through breath and silence). Big staring eyes. Body is a solid mass wrapped in robes. The opposite of the Oil Warrior's curves: he's geometric, mountain-like.

Default — immovable, breathing
Attack — force exhale blast

Cacao Dealer

Hunched, leaning in conspiratorially. Everything about him suggests "guy you don't want to buy from but you will." Long coat hiding his wares. Eyes are never fully visible — hat brim or angle always obscures them. Thin, wiry build. Hands always doing something shady.

Default — coat open, beckoning
After sale — coat closed, hands rubbing

What Makes This Scalable

The key insight: each character has a distinct body shape language.

Steve = vertical lines, right angles, narrow. He's a spreadsheet.
Oil Warrior = curves, flowing lines, wide skirt. She's a pour of essential oil.
Breathwork Monk = triangle, solid mass, wide base. He's a mountain.
Cacao Dealer = diagonal lines, hunched angles. He's an open trench coat.

To add a new character, you just ask: "what shape IS this person?" A yoga instructor is a pretzel. A crystal healer is a cluster of angles. A drum circle leader is a big round belly with tiny limbs. The shape does 80% of the character work, then you add one signature prop and one face expression.

Expression system: Each character needs only 4–5 face states (neutral, happy, angry, scared, special). The body can do a few poses (idle, attack, hit, talk). Mix and match = dozens of visual states from a small set of parts.

For Godot: Each character could be a scene with swappable face/arm/prop nodes. Animate with Tweens. No sprite sheets needed — just SVG or simple vector art that Godot renders natively.
