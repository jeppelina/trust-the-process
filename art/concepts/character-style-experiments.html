Character Style Experiments

Five visual directions for Burner Bash — same characters, different approaches. Which one feels right?

Style 1 — Stick Figures + Props

The West of Loathing approach. Bodies are nearly identical — personality lives in accessories, posture, and one or two signature details. Dead simple to produce. Easy to animate (tilt, bounce, shake). Scales to dozens of characters by just designing hats and held items.

Steve (Accountant)
Essential Oil Warrior
Breathwork Monk

Style 2 — Glyph / Rune Characters

Characters are abstract symbols — like ancient pictograms or bathroom-sign people, but with personality. Fits the "spiritual" theme perfectly (they look like they belong on a temple wall). Very fast to create. Animate by scaling, rotating, glowing. Each character IS their symbol.

Steve (square, rigid)
Oil Warrior (flowing, wavy)
Breathwork Monk (concentric)
Cacao Dealer (jagged, shifty)

Style 3 — Silhouettes with Accent Color

Solid black shapes (or dark brown) with one signature colored element per character. High contrast, very readable at small sizes, easy to produce in bulk. Personality comes from the silhouette outline and the one colored detail. Think "shadow puppet theater" — which honestly fits the spiritual journey theme beautifully.

Steve
Essential Oil Warrior
Breathwork Monk

Style 4 — Typographic Characters

Characters are built from letters, punctuation, and text symbols. Leans hard into the "text-based game" identity. Each character's name or nature literally becomes their visual form. Very on-brand for a game where writing IS the art. Could animate by shuffling/rearranging the letters.

Steve (numbers & currency)
Oil Warrior (tildes & flowers)
Monk (circles & breath)
Cacao Dealer (brackets & boxes)

Style 5 — Emoji Portraits (Evolved)

Take your current emoji approach and push it further — not just a single emoji, but a composed "emoji portrait" in a frame. Surprisingly expressive, zero drawing skill needed, and it actually reinforces the satirical tone (these characters ARE their stereotypes). Fastest to produce by far. Could animate frames/borders.

Comparison Notes

Style Speed to make Animate? Scales to 50+ chars? Comedy fit
Stick + Props Fast Easy (tween bones) Great — share body, vary props ★★★★★
Glyphs / Runes Medium Pulse, glow, rotate Good — each is unique symbol ★★★★
Silhouettes Medium Limited (slide, shake) OK — outlines take some thought ★★★
Typographic Very fast Shuffle, rearrange letters Excellent — just pick new symbols ★★★★
Emoji Evolved Fastest Frame effects, shake Excellent — infinite emoji combos ★★★★
