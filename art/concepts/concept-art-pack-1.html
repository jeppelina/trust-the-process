Concept Art Pack 1

New characters, a scene composition, and a combat UI mockup. All SVG, all using the established visual language. Coordinates map directly to Godot _draw() calls.

The Founder — Zone 1 Boss

Triangle shape, wide base — a mountain you're supposed to climb. Barefoot, linen everything, man-bun. Arms often open in a perpetual "welcome" gesture. Eyes are warm but calculating. He's bigger than every other character — he looms. The triangle silhouette says: immovable, important, rooted. His accent color is deep warm amber — the gold of a sunset retreat brochure.

Default — arms wide, "welcome home"
Phase 2 — mask slipping, mala breaking
Phase 3 — just Paul, scared and small

Shape note: The Founder is 150-180px tall in-game (boss scale). His triangle silhouette collapses across phases — wide-open arms → defensive → hugging himself. The mala beads breaking is his visual thread: whole → snapping → scattered on the ground.

The Oversharer — Zone 1 Regular Enemy

Circles and bursting energy. She's an emotional explosion given form — round head, round eyes, round mouth, arms always reaching toward you. Tears are a permanent fixture. Her shape says: no edges, no containment, everything spills out. She's invading your personal space and her silhouette shows it — she LEANS at you.

Default — reaching, crying, talking
Trauma Dump — words as weapons

Combat note: The trauma dump attack is visualized as literal words flying out of her mouth. In Godot, these can be small Label nodes that fly toward Steve with physics. Her journal pages scatter on crit hits.

The Competitive Meditator — Zone 1 Regular Enemy

A perfect circle. She's so centered she's become a sphere. Always in lotus position — even when she shouldn't be. The smuggest silhouette in the game: perfectly balanced, perfectly still, radiating an aura of "I'm better at being calm than you." Her mala beads are bigger than The Founder's. Her cushion is nicer than anyone's.

Default — perfect lotus, smug aura
Performative Stillness — peeking, untouchable

Her "one peeking eye" is key — she's always watching to see if people notice how good she is at not caring. The defense aura can pulse in Godot using a simple scale tween on the circle radii.

Groundskeeper Frank — Zone 1 NPC / Ally

The only normal person here. Square. Literally — his shape language is the rectangle. Sturdy, no-nonsense, built like a toolbox. Baseball cap, work boots, always carrying something practical. He's the straight man in a world of curves and triangles. His accent color is tool gray — the least spiritual color in the game. He sells Steve actual coffee from a maintenance closet.

Default — wrench, coffee, unimpressed
Talking — side-eye, giving directions

Frank's rectangle shape language is deliberate — he's the only character in the game built from straight lines AND right angles. Steve is straight lines but nervous/narrow. Frank is straight lines and SOLID. They share a visual language because they share a worldview.

Scene: The Welcome Pavilion

Full scene composition at game viewport scale (960×640). Shows the HUD, scene area with characters on the ground line, and dialogue panel. This is what the player sees when they first arrive at The Sanctuary.

960×640 viewport. Characters scaled at 60-70% in scene view. Ground line at y=350. Prayer flags add color without breaking the muted palette. Frank is always in the background, watching. The Oversharer is already reaching toward where Steve would be standing.

Combat UI: Steve vs. The Essential Oil Warrior

Combat layout concept. Turn-based, with Steve on the left and the enemy on the right. Action menu at the bottom replaces the dialogue panel. Damage numbers float. Status effects shown as small icons near health bars.

Combat actions map to the four button layout: Attack (physical/briefcase), Skills (accounting abilities), Items (office supplies and loot), Flee (social excuses). The flee button text changes per enemy — against the Oversharer it'd be "I Have A Meeting," against the Yoga Snob it'd be "My Hamstrings Say No."
