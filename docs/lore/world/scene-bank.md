# Scene Idea Bank

A running collection of scene, moment, and encounter concepts. Each entry is raw inspiration — not yet assigned to a zone. Draw from here when designing quests, ambient moments, cutscenes, or zone events.

---

### The Cacao Ceremony That Goes Wrong
Everyone drinks ceremonial cacao and is supposed to "open their hearts." One person has a full psychotic break. The facilitator insists this is "exactly what's supposed to happen." Steve watches in horror as nobody calls a doctor because "Western medicine wouldn't understand."

### The Sharing Circle That Gets Too Real
A round of performative vulnerability until one person accidentally shares something genuinely devastating. The room goes silent. Nobody knows what to do because the format only works for curated pain. Steve — the least spiritual person present — is the only one who reacts like a human.

### Steve Finds the Back Office
Behind the spiritual facade is a completely normal office with marketing spreadsheets, customer acquisition costs, and an influencer outreach strategy. The contrast between "sacred community" and "B2C wellness brand with a 40% profit margin" is the scene.

### The 2 AM Dorm Meltdown
Someone in Steve's dorm has a breakthrough/breakdown at 2 AM. Sobbing, primal sounds, possibly chanting. Steve lies in his bunk staring at the ceiling. Other roommates pretend to sleep. One whispers "this is beautiful" in the dark. Steve considers sleeping in his car.

### The Fire Pit Where Everyone Gossips
The fire pit is supposed to be for "sacred presence." In reality it's where everyone gossips about the facilitators, the food, and each other — but using spiritual framing so it doesn't count. "I'm not judging, I'm just holding an observation about Celestia's energy lately."

### Steve Accidentally Has a Real Moment
During a breathwork session or a quiet moment in nature, something genuinely shifts for Steve. He feels something. It scares him more than any enemy. He immediately suppresses it and pretends it didn't happen. The game acknowledges it with a small stat change the player might not notice.

### The Gift Shop Economy
A gift shop selling crystals at 4000% markup, books by The Founder, branded merchandise, and "intention candles" at $45 each. The Karma Yogi running it has dead eyes and a radiant smile. Everything is a "suggested donation" that is actually a fixed price. Steve finds the wholesale invoices in a drawer labeled "DO NOT OPEN (sacred)."

### The Celebrity Guest's "Authentic" Photo Shoot
A minor influencer doing 40 takes of a "spontaneous" moment of meditation. Steve holds a crystal near her face for lighting. The caption will read "Day 3. No phone. No screens. Just me and the earth." She is on her phone. She has always been on her phone.

### The Volunteer Wall of "Completion"
A wall of photos showing Karma Yogis who have "completed their seva." The photos are presented warmly but it's essentially a wall of people who escaped. Some photos have been there for years. Nobody talks about where they went. One photo looks like it was removed and put back.

### The Menu That Attacks You
Every meal is an ordeal. Dinner is "a turmeric-ashwagandha-reishi broth infused with loving intention." There is no salt. There is no flavor. Someone asks for salt and three people stage an intervention about their "sodium attachment." Steve finds actual coffee being sold out of a maintenance closet by the groundskeeper. It's the black market economy of the retreat.

### The Workshop Schedule as a Puzzle
The workshop schedule is intentionally overlapping so guests can't comparison-shop. Figuring out the real schedule — who teaches when, which rooms are actually available — is a literal puzzle Steve has to solve using Schedule Optimization.

### The Founder's TED Talk Rehearsal
Steve accidentally walks in on The Founder rehearsing a TED talk in front of a mirror. The talk is called "The Courage to Be Ordinary" and it's being given by a man who changed his name, built a cult, and hasn't done his own laundry in eight years.

### The Sweat Lodge Without a Permit
An underground (literally and figuratively) ceremony happening in an unpermitted structure. Someone passes out from the heat and the facilitator says "they're journeying." Steve's Risk Assessment ability flags multiple OSHA violations.

### The Night Walk
Steve walks the grounds at night. He sees: a facilitator sneaking to The Founder's cabin, two Karma Yogis having a whispered conversation about escaping, someone meditating alone who might actually be having a real spiritual experience, and a beautiful sky that Steve grudgingly admits is nice. A rare quiet scene.

### The Exit Interview
When someone tries to leave The Sanctuary, they're put through a "completion ceremony" that is functionally an exit interview designed to make them stay. Three people surround the leaver and ask "what's really coming up for you?" until the person either cries, gives up, or — if Steve helps — actually walks out.

### The Parking Lot Conversation
Two guests have the most honest conversation in the game in the parking lot, next to their cars, right before one of them leaves. Away from the "container" they speak normally. "Is any of this real?" "Some of it. Maybe. I don't know." "Yeah." It's the most human moment in the zone.

### Steve Reads His Wife's Journal Entry
Found in The Founder's files or in her old dorm. A journal entry from early in her stay. She writes about missing Steve, about feeling something she can't name, about being terrified and excited at the same time. It's loving, confused, and honest. Steve reads it and doesn't know what to feel. The player doesn't get a dialogue choice here. Just silence.

### The Guru's Origin Story Reveal
Late in a zone, Steve discovers the boss's real backstory — always mundane, always sad. The Founder is Derek from Scottsdale whose marriage fell apart. The Breathwork Master is an ex-accountant (Steve's parallel) who had a real breakdown. The Tantra Teacher was cheated on and rebuilt their identity around sexual empowerment. The reveal always complicates the fight.

### Burning Man Arrival
The final zone opener. Steve drives through the desert. The landscape changes. Dust everywhere. A greeter in body paint hugs him and says "Welcome home." Steve has never felt less at home. His Honda Civic looks like a spaceship from another dimension. Someone immediately asks if he's brought any gifts. He has a bag of office supplies. They're thrilled.
