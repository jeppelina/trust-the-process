# Setting Overview

## The World

The game takes place across a network of spiritual retreats, workshops, festivals, and communities — the real-world pipeline that takes a normal person from "maybe I should try yoga" to "I live in a van and my name is Starweaver now."

Each location is a zone in the game, escalating in intensity and absurdity.

## Zone Progression (Draft)

1. **The Yoga Studio** — Entry point. Gentle, suburban. Your first contact with "the world." Enemies: Overzealous instructors, MLM essential oil sellers, the guy who does headstands during savasana.

2. **The Meditation Retreat** — Silence is mandatory. Communication through gestures and passive-aggression. Boss: The Guru Who Won't Stop Talking About Not Talking.

3. **The Breathwork Workshop** — Things get weird. People crying, screaming, having "visions." Steve accidentally has a genuine emotional release and is horrified.

4. **The Healing Arts Center** — Crystal healers, reiki masters, sound bathers. Everything is "medicine." Your lunch is medicine. Your crystals are medicine. Your $300 session is definitely medicine.

5. **The Tantra Temple** — Sex positivity cranked to maximum. Consent workshops that last longer than the activities they're about. Steve has never been more uncomfortable.

6. **The Ayahuasca Ceremony** — Where Steve's wife was last seen as "herself." Deep jungle or suspicious suburban house with tapestries. The shaman has a Venmo.

7. **The Men's Work Camp** — Drums. So many drums. Primal screaming. Vulnerability as competitive sport. "I cried harder than you, bro."

8. **The Poly House** — A group house where everyone is dating everyone and the Google Calendar is the most complex system in the game.

9. **The Festival Circuit** — Regional burns, transformational festivals. Glitter as environmental hazard. Art cars as enemy vehicles.

10. **Burning Man** — The final zone. The playa. Everything converges here. Steve's wife is running a camp called "The Heart Portal" and she's never been happier. Or has she?

## The Spiritual-Industrial Complex

The world has an economy based on:
- **Cacao coins** — Primary currency
- **Insight points** — XP, gained by surviving spiritual experiences
- **Gratitude tokens** — Given by NPCs, used for side quest rewards
- **Actual money** — Steve's accounting salary, which is absurdly powerful but frowned upon
