# Zone 1 Quests: The Sanctuary

## Main Quest: "Where Is My Wife?"

### Objective
Find out where Steve's wife went after The Sanctuary.

### Quest Flow

**Step 1: Arrive and Register**
Steve arrives thinking this is a spa weekend. The Welcome Pavilion Karma Yogi checks him in, takes his phone ("digital detox is part of the container"), assigns him a spiritual name, and gives him the orientation pamphlet. He's assigned to a dorm. His roommate is already crying.

**Step 2: Ask Around**
Nobody gives straight answers. Steve asks about his wife and gets:
- "I don't feel called to share about other people's journeys"
- "Have you considered that your need to find her is actually about finding yourself?"
- "She mentioned you. She said she felt... constrained."
Every NPC remembers her but wraps the information in spiritual deflection. Steve has to earn Insight to get them to talk to him properly.

**Step 3: Gain Insight (The Grind)**
Steve must participate in activities to raise his Insight level enough for people to take him seriously. This means:
- Attending a yoga class (combat encounter with Yoga Snob if he does it wrong)
- Surviving a sharing circle (endurance challenge — don't react for 10 minutes)
- Doing a "work exchange" shift in the kitchen (the Karma Yogi underworld)

**Step 4: The Kitchen Connection**
In the kitchen, Steve meets the Secretly Skeptical Karma Yogi, who actually liked his wife and is willing to talk — but not here. They arrange to meet at the fire pit after the evening ceremony.

**Step 5: Fire Pit Intel**
The Skeptical Yogi reveals: Steve's wife got close to The Founder. Private sessions. She changed fast. Then she left with a recommendation letter. The info about where she went is in The Founder's records, in the Back Office or the Ceremony Space.

**Step 6: Getting Access**
The Back Office is locked. The Ceremony Space requires Gatekeeper approval. Steve has to either:
- **Path A:** Sneak into the Back Office at night (stealth challenge, risk of getting caught by the Facilitator Diva who sleeps there sometimes)
- **Path B:** Convince the Gatekeeper (requires high Insight OR completing the Gatekeeper's humiliating task)
- **Path C:** The Local Groundskeeper has a master key (requires completing their side quest)

**Step 7: Confront The Founder (Boss Fight)**
See boss design document. Multiple outcomes.

**Step 8: Get the Lead**
Steve obtains the recommendation letter and/or records showing where his wife was sent. Zone complete.

## Side Quest 1: "Free Labor Isn't Seva"

### Quest Giver: The Secretly Skeptical Karma Yogi
**Setup:** They're trapped. They signed a 6-month "commitment" that's not legally binding but socially enforced. They want to leave but their passport/ID is "being held safely" by the office (red flag). They ask Steve for help.

**Objectives:**
1. Retrieve the Karma Yogi's documents from the Back Office filing cabinet
2. Get them to the Local Groundskeeper who can drive them to the bus station
3. Optional: Confront the office manager about the document-holding practice

**Reward:**
- The Karma Yogi gives Steve their work schedule (evidence item for boss fight)
- The Groundskeeper becomes a reliable ally
- XP / Insight

**Comedy beats:**
- The filing cabinet is labeled "Sacred Documents of Belonging"
- Other Karma Yogis try to guilt-trip the escapee: "You're abandoning the community" / "This is your ego running"
- The escapee has a panic attack about leaving and Steve has to talk them through it using corporate HR language, which somehow works perfectly

## Side Quest 2: "The Crystal Audit"

### Quest Giver: A Paying Guest (frustrated)
**Setup:** A guest paid $300 for a "custom crystal healing session" and received a bag of rocks from the gift shop with a markup of 4000%. They want proof they were scammed.

**Objectives:**
1. Investigate the Healing Hut's crystal inventory
2. Find the wholesale receipts (crystals bought in bulk from Alibaba for $0.50 each)
3. Present evidence to the guest

**Reward:**
- The Really Big Crystal (weapon)
- Cacao coins
- The guest becomes disillusioned and leaves (minor karmic consequence?)

**Comedy beats:**
- The Healing Hut practitioner explains that the value isn't in the crystal, it's in the "intentional programming" they do (they whisper at the crystals)
- Steve finds a laptop with an open Alibaba tab showing bulk crystal orders
- The wholesale receipt is taped to the inside of a cabinet labeled "DO NOT OPEN (sacred)"

## Side Quest 3: "The Influencer's Dilemma"

### Quest Giver: The Celebrity Guest (minor influencer)
**Setup:** They're supposed to be on a "digital detox" but they need to post content or they lose a brand deal. They've smuggled in a phone but can't get WiFi. They've heard The Founder's cabin has WiFi.

**Objectives:**
1. Help the influencer get the WiFi password (Steve might already have it from exploring)
2. Stand guard while they post their "authentic disconnection" content
3. Don't get caught by the facilitators who enforce the no-phone rule

**Reward:**
- The influencer promotes Steve's "brand" (gives him a +charisma buff that works in future zones among younger NPCs)
- Wi-Fi Password item (if Steve doesn't have it already)
- Cacao coins

**Comedy beats:**
- The influencer's "authentic" posts are hilariously staged: them meditating, them laughing in nature, them holding a crystal with the caption "learning to just... be"
- All photos are heavily filtered
- They do 40 takes of a "spontaneous" moment
- If Steve looks at their follower count it's... 4,200. They call themselves a "micro-influencer."

## Side Quest 4: "The Groundskeeper's Wisdom"

### Quest Giver: Local Groundskeeper
**Setup:** The Groundskeeper needs help fixing something practical — a burst pipe, a broken generator, a fence repair. None of the spiritual people can or will help because manual labor isn't "their calling" (unless it's called seva and someone else does it).

**Objectives:**
1. Help fix the practical problem (mini-game or simple fetch quest)
2. Listen to the Groundskeeper's commentary on the retreat (optional dialogue, very funny)
3. Get the master key as a reward

**Reward:**
- Master key (opens Back Office — alternate path for main quest)
- The Groundskeeper's respect
- Practical item (tool that works as a weapon)
- Best dialogue in the zone

**Comedy beats:**
- The Groundskeeper has watched 12 years of retreats. They've Seen Things.
- They refer to The Founder by his real name (Derek) and nobody else even knows his real name
- They have zero spiritual vocabulary but accidentally say the wisest things in the game
- Steve and the Groundskeeper bond over being the only normal people here
- The Groundskeeper's toolbox is the most functional item in a place full of crystals and singing bowls

## Optional Encounters (Random / Exploration)

**The Sharing Circle**
Steve accidentally joins a sharing circle and has to survive without reacting. Turn-based endurance challenge where each NPC shares something increasingly intense and Steve has to choose responses. Wrong responses trigger combat.

**Night Patrol**
Wandering the grounds at night, Steve encounters Karma Yogis doing late-night "processing" (gossiping about the facilitators), a facilitator sneaking to the Founder's cabin, and possibly a genuinely beautiful moment of nature that Steve grudgingly appreciates.

**The Merchandise Table**
A Karma Yogi is selling The Founder's self-published book at a table. If Steve examines it, he finds it's 200 pages of quotes misattributed to Rumi and Buddha, plus stock photos. Buying it unlocks special dialogue options with The Founder (you can quote his own book at him).
