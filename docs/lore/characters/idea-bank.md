# Character Idea Bank

A running collection of character concepts. Each entry is raw inspiration — not yet assigned to a zone or role. Draw from here when designing new zones, enemies, NPCs, bosses, or companions.

---

### The Shaman with a Venmo
A ceremony leader running ayahuasca sessions in a suspicious suburban house with tapestries on the walls. Charges premium rates. Venmo handle is something like @CosmicMedicine. Has a waiver form that's longer than the ceremony. "The medicine will find you. Also please initial page 4 regarding liability."

### The Consent Workshop Facilitator
Runs consent workshops that last longer than the activities they're about. Three-hour presentation on how to ask someone if you can hug them. Hands out laminated cards. Deeply well-intentioned but the bureaucracy of intimacy has become its own form of paralysis.

### The Guy Who Brings a Didgeridoo Everywhere
Every gathering. Every circle. Every meal. The didgeridoo is there. Nobody asked him to play. He plays anyway. Claims it "clears the energy." It does not clear the energy. It makes the energy worse.

### The Polyamory Google Calendar Administrator
Lives in the poly house. Their Google Calendar is the most complex system in the game — color-coded by partner, with recurring events, buffer time between dates, and a shared spreadsheet tracking "relationship energy allocation." More organized than Steve's accounting firm.

### The Raw Vegan Chef
Runs the kitchen at a retreat. Everything is raw, dehydrated, or "activated." Has never used an oven. Calls cooking "thermal violence against nutrients." The food looks beautiful on Instagram and tastes like sadness.

### The Primal Scream Therapist
Leads sessions where people scream in a field for two hours. Claims it releases "ancestral trauma stored in the jaw." The neighbors have called the police four times. He considers this a compliment.

### The Retired Banker Turned Breathwork Facilitator
Left Goldman Sachs after a panic attack that he later reframed as a "kundalini awakening." Now charges $300/session for the same hyperventilation technique. Still checks his portfolio every morning but calls it "abundance tracking."

### The Ecstatic Dance DJ
Takes the no-talking rule extremely seriously. Communicates entirely through eye contact and interpretive movement. Has been "in flow" for so long they've forgotten how to have a normal conversation. Their Spotify playlists have names like "Surrender Vol. 17."

### The Burning Man Camp Leader
Runs a theme camp with a 40-page PDF of rules about "radical gifting." Has a tier system for camp members based on how much they donated. Talks about "decommodification" while running what is functionally a luxury glamping operation at $2,500/week.

### The Men's Work Facilitator
Drums. So many drums. Competitive vulnerability. "I cried harder than you, bro." Holds space for "the masculine wound" while maintaining the most fragile ego in any room. His "warrior training" weekend costs $800 and involves yelling at trees.

### The Crystal Influencer
4,200 Instagram followers. Calls themselves a "micro-influencer." Posts daily photos of crystals with captions about "high-vibe living." Has spent $12,000 on crystals and earned $340 from affiliate links. Considers this "investing in my brand."

### The Somatic Therapist Who Diagnoses Everyone
Walks around reading people's bodies uninvited. "Your left shoulder is holding your mother's grief." "Your hip flexors are screaming." "I can see your divorce in your posture." Technically skilled but has zero boundaries about when to deploy it.

### The "I Don't Label Myself" Person
Rejects all labels while having the most specific identity imaginable. "I'm not a healer, I'm a space-holder. I'm not polyamorous, I'm relationship-fluid. I'm not a coach, I'm a catalytic mirror." Has 14 different modalities listed in their Instagram bio.

### The Festival Veteran
Been to 47 Burning Mans (they count). Judges newcomers for not understanding the culture while gatekeeping every aspect of it. "Back in '98 it was REAL." Hasn't had a permanent address in 15 years. Lives in a converted school bus named "Shakti."

### The Sound Healer with Military-Grade Equipment
Has $20,000 worth of crystal singing bowls, gongs, and tuning forks. The volume is medically concerning. Claims specific frequencies cure specific diseases. The vibrations have cracked a window twice. Insurance won't cover the studio anymore.

### The Couple Who "Opened Their Relationship" at a Retreat
Came as a couple to "deepen their bond." One of them "opened up" during a tantra workshop. Now they're navigating polyamory with the emotional maturity of two people who read one book about it on the drive home. It's going badly. They're both pretending it isn't.

### The Local Who's Seen Everything
Works at the gas station / diner / hardware store near the retreat center. Has watched 15 years of spiritual tourists cycle through. Has the driest, most accurate commentary on the whole ecosystem. Calls everyone "honey" and sees through every facade instantly.

### The Karma Yogi Lifer
Has been volunteering at retreats for 6 years straight. Has no savings, no fixed address, and no exit plan. Fully identifies with the role. Gets anxious when not serving. The system has completely absorbed them and they call it "devotion."

### Mattis — The Fallen Viking (Multi-Zone Arc Character)
A potential recurring character or tragic NPC arc that spans the whole game. When Steve first encounters Mattis he's basically an Alexander Skarsgård-type — gritty, handsome alpha energy, good money, magnetic presence. The kind of guy who walks into a retreat and every facilitator wants to "work with him." Then he meets Sanna, a succubus-like woman who draws him in but can never actually be intimate with him — she has pain during sex, but apparently only with Mattis. She can and does sleep with everyone else. The humiliation and obsession pulls Mattis deeper and deeper into the spiritual pipeline, chasing healing modalities to "fix" himself (since the narrative becomes that HE's the problem). Each zone Steve visits, Mattis has been there too, but further deteriorated. By the final chapter, Mattis is a drooling shell on a bicycle with a harmonica in his mouth — the "free man" archetype taken to its logical horror. He's homeless, completely broken, and the community calls him "liberated." He is the game's darkest cautionary tale about what happens when the pipeline fully consumes someone. Sanna is never seen — only referenced. She's always somewhere else, thriving.

### Sanna — The Absent Succubus
Mattis's destroyer. Never physically present in the game — only referenced by other characters. She has a condition that causes pain during sex, but specifically and exclusively with Mattis. With everyone else she's fine. This drives Mattis insane and into the spiritual world seeking answers. Every zone has someone who mentions her casually: "Oh Sanna? She was here last month. Amazing energy. Really open." The ambiguity of whether she's deliberately cruel or genuinely struggling is never resolved. She might be a boss in a DLC.

### The Single Mom in the Tech Collective (NPC / Tragic Background)
Left behind by the father of her child — he had to go "find himself" in India. It's the second kid he's abandoned to go seeking. She ended up in a "tech collective" that was supposed to be community, a modern family, friends who get it. In reality she's more alone than anyone. The baby screams while the collective — a bunch of 33-year-olds sleeping in bunk beds because someone convinced them it was revolutionary — ignores her to worship tech gurus on their laptops. The bunk beds are key. Adults. In bunk beds. Voluntarily. They think it's "intentional living." She knows it's just poverty with a philosophy. Could be an NPC Steve meets between zones, maybe at a co-living space. She's the most clear-eyed person in any room but trapped by circumstance. Possible ally or quest-giver.

### Brother Björn — The Gentle Giant Monk
Huge. Bald. Hagrid-like. Former military, physics dropout, ongoing mental health struggles he manages with sheer force of gentleness. Desperately wants to be Gandalf — the wise elder who speaks in parables and everyone respects. In reality he's a sweet, broken man who overcompensates through social relating games and "circling" (a practice where people sit in a circle and describe what they notice about each other in real time). He's REALLY into circling. He'll circle with anyone. He'll circle with Steve in a parking lot. His observations are sometimes accidentally profound and sometimes completely unhinged. "I notice that your polo shirt is holding a lot of grief, Steve." Could be a companion — high HP tank who fights with bear hugs and unsolicited observations. His combat ability "I Notice That..." applies random buffs or debuffs depending on what he notices.

### The Inspirational Speakers (Enemy Type / Mini-Boss Tier)
The real alphas of this world. They have the charisma to fool even normal people — not just the already-converted. They're public speakers who take genuinely interesting philosophical ideas and flatten them into bumper sticker wisdom delivered with TED Talk energy. "Did you realize that someone MADE UP the driving license?" "Money is just a collective hallucination." "Time doesn't exist — we invented it." Each statement is technically adjacent to real philosophy but stripped of all nuance. They weaponize the Dunning-Kruger effect. In combat they're dangerous because their Charisma stat is so high that even Steve's Normality defense wavers. Their attacks include "Mind Blown" (AoE psychic damage), "Reframe Reality" (confuses target), and "Viral Clip" (summons followers who saw their Instagram reel). Weakness: asking follow-up questions. "Okay but what specifically do you mean by that?" causes them to buffer like a loading screen.

### The Sacred Norm-Breakers (Enemy Type)
People who think it's cute and revolutionary that they don't follow social norms. Dancing strange, vaguely sexual dances in public spaces — in parks, at markets, on public transport — fully convinced they're "healing the world" through their embodied freedom. They see themselves as brave artists. Everyone around them sees a person doing pelvic thrusts at a farmers market. They form packs. They encourage each other. "You're so FREE." "Your body is a PRAYER." Steve's presence — a man in a polo shirt who follows every social norm — is physically painful to them. In combat their attacks are all movement-based: "Ecstatic Expression" (cringe damage), "Public Undulation" (AoE discomfort), "You Should Try It" (attempts to make Steve dance — if successful, Normality drops significantly). Weakness: any authority figure or a child asking "what is that man doing?"

### The Crowdfunders (Enemy Type / Possible Mini-Boss)
A whole ecosystem of people who are so bad at sustaining themselves that they constantly need "community" to bail them out via crowdfunding. There are several tiers. The **basic crowdfunder** launches a GoFundMe for a doomed project — a "conscious chocolate brand" or a "healing travel podcast" — that anyone with basic business sense can see will fail. The **crisis crowdfunder** made an avoidable catastrophic decision (like traveling internationally with a terminally ill friend without insurance, or investing their rent money in a sound healing certification) and now needs emergency funds. The **professional crowdfunder** is the mini-boss variant — they've turned crowdfunding into a lifestyle. Every six months there's a new campaign: a "transformational training in India" (they don't go), a "community art project" (they keep the money), a "medical emergency" (it's a nose job framed as "somatic restructuring"). Steve's Audit ability is devastating here — it reveals their campaign history and completion rate (0%). In combat they attack by making Steve feel guilty for having savings. "Must be nice to have FINANCIAL PRIVILEGE, Steve."

### The Friendless Radical Includer (Enemy Type / Tragic Minion)
Shows up at every party, every gathering, every orgy — because "radical inclusion" means nobody can tell them to leave. They take advantage of communities that have explicitly committed to welcoming everyone. Nobody calls them a friend. Nobody invites them specifically. They just... appear. Usually mentally unstable in ways the community isn't equipped to handle but won't acknowledge because acknowledging it would mean setting a boundary and boundaries conflict with the inclusion ethos. They're at every event with a slightly too-intense smile and nobody's phone number. In combat they're a tragic minion — low HP, attacks that are more sad than threatening ("Can I Come Too?" forces Steve to include them in his party temporarily, "I Thought We Were Friends" guilt damage). Steve can help them or fight them. Helping them is the morally right thing but the game makes it genuinely difficult because they don't make it easy to help.

### The Excel Master Gone Feral
A former corporate drone — maybe even an accountant like Steve — who snapped and went full primal. He discovered "masculine embodiment" and now thinks he's a warrior. Thinks he's strong (he's not bad, but he learned martial arts from YouTube). Thinks he's sexy (debatable). LOVES spanking — it's his "practice," his "edge work," his "somatic integration through impact." He talks about spreadsheets and quarterly reports in the same breath as BDSM and breathwork. He is Steve's dark mirror — what Steve could become if the spiritual pipeline gets him. In combat he's unpredictable, switching between corporate jargon attacks and primal screaming. His skill "Pivot Table of Pain" is both a spreadsheet reference and a wrestling move. Steve is deeply unsettled by this man because he recognizes himself in there somewhere.

### The Rich Enablers (NPC Type / Economy Mechanic)
Wealthy men (usually) who orbit the community handing out money. On the surface it's "generosity" and "abundance." In practice, by funding women's doomed projects, retreats, and lifestyles, they maintain those women's poverty in a vicious cycle — because why get a job when someone will pay for your next cacao ceremony certification? The money always comes with access — to attention, to intimacy, to sex with the desperate. They never call it transactional. It's "supporting the feminine." It's "investing in potential." The women never call it dependency. It's "receiving abundance." Steve's Audit ability reveals the pattern instantly: money flows from Rich Enabler to attractive woman, woman launches failed project, money runs out, woman needs more money, Rich Enabler is there again. Could function as a zone mechanic — a side quest where Steve has to trace the money and reveal the cycle to someone trapped in it. The Rich Enabler is hard to fight because technically they haven't done anything illegal. They've just built a system where generosity is a leash.

### The Tantra Reverend Mother — The Bene Gesserit of Burning Man (Major NPC / Boss / Faction Leader)

**The Concept:** A woman who runs the tantra scene across the entire hippie-spiritual pipeline — retreats, festivals, Burning Man — as a multi-generational breeding program disguised as sacred sexuality workshops. She is the Reverend Mother of a secret order called the **Bene Tantra** (or publicly, "The Sisterhood of the Felt Sense"). They don't call it a breeding program. They call it "Conscious Conception" — the belief that if you control the exact spiritual, sexual, and astrological conditions of conception, you can birth an enlightened being. The Kwisatz Haderach equivalent: **The Tantric Messiah** — referred to in whispered tones as "The One Who Breathes For All." A being of perfect energetic alignment who will unite all modalities — yoga, breathwork, plant medicine, sound healing — into a single unified consciousness. The Bene Tantra have been trying to breed this being for decades.

**Her Name:** Something like **Reverend Mother Solene** — or she insists on being called simply "Mother." She has a more mundane name she's buried (maybe Karen, or something devastatingly ordinary). Senior members of her order are called **Say'yadinas** (a direct Dune lift that nobody in the community recognizes because none of them have read actual books).

**The Breeding Program:** The Bene Tantra maintain detailed genealogical records — not on paper, but encoded in astrological charts, Human Design readings, and "energetic lineage maps" that look like conspiracy boards. They track "high-vibration bloodlines" — people whose ancestors were shamans, healers, or "old souls." The program works by steering specific men and women together through "conscious matchmaking workshops" at retreats and festivals. The women in the order are trained in **The Tantric Voice** — the ability to use breath, eye contact, and precise micro-touch to make anyone do what they want. It's not supernatural. It's just extremely advanced social manipulation dressed up as sacred intimacy. Think: NLP, attachment theory exploitation, and weaponized vulnerability, all wrapped in candlelight and sandalwood.

**The Voice (Tantric Version):** In Dune, the Bene Gesserit Voice is a command that cannot be disobeyed. Here, the Bene Tantra equivalent is **The Breath** — a specific breathing pattern combined with sustained eye contact and calibrated touch that puts the target in a deeply suggestible state. They call it "opening someone's heart space." Steve would call it "being manipulated by someone who smells like ylang ylang." In gameplay terms, The Breath could be a status effect that forces Steve to skip a turn, lower his Normality, or make a choice he didn't intend to make.

**The Spice → The Cacao:** In Dune, spice melange grants prescience and extends life. Here, the equivalent is a special ceremonial cacao blend that the Bene Tantra brew exclusively — stronger than normal cacao, laced with adaptogenic herbs and possibly microdoses of something less legal. They claim it "opens the womb chakra" and is essential for Conscious Conception. It's served at every Bene Tantra ceremony. The recipe is a closely guarded secret. Regular cacao dealers (like the one in Zone 1) are unknowing supply chain nodes for the order.

**The Gom Jabbar Test:** The Bene Gesserit test humanity by making you hold your hand in a pain box. The Bene Tantra test "readiness" through a ceremony called **The Held Space** — you sit in a pitch-dark room while senior members take turns saying the most emotionally devastating observations about you, drawn from intake questionnaires you filled out thinking they were for "personalization." If you react — cry, leave, argue — you fail. If you sit in silence and "receive," you pass. It's essentially weaponized therapy. Steve's Normality is both his weakness (he might crack) and his strength (he's too emotionally repressed to react, which they interpret as spiritual mastery).

**Her Role in the Game:**
- **Zone 2-3 NPC:** First appears running a tantra workshop that Steve accidentally walks into. She immediately identifies Steve as interesting — not because he's spiritually gifted, but because his bloodline (his wife's family tree, specifically) is one she's been tracking. Steve's wife didn't end up in the pipeline by accident. The Bene Tantra steered her there.
- **Mid-Game Reveal:** Steve discovers his wife was specifically targeted — matched with a "high-vibration partner" at a retreat, guided through escalating workshops, eventually directed toward The Sanctuary (Zone 1) as a staging ground. The Founder (Paul) is a Bene Tantra puppet. He thinks he's in charge. He is not.
- **Late-Game Boss:** Reverend Mother Solene is a major boss fight. She doesn't fight physically — she fights with The Breath, emotional manipulation, and summoning loyal followers. Her attacks include "Conscious Uncoupling" (attempts to sever Steve's motivation to find his wife), "The Held Space" (forces Steve to endure multi-turn psychological damage without acting), and "Bloodline Audit" (reveals uncomfortable truths about Steve's family that lower Normality). Her weakness: Steve's sheer normality. The Breath doesn't work well on someone who thinks essential oils are "just smells."

**The Order's Hierarchy:**
- **Initiates:** Women (and some men) in early training. Run basic tantra workshops. Think they're learning self-empowerment. Are actually being evaluated for the breeding program.
- **Say'yadinas:** Senior members who have "passed through The Held Space." Conduct Conscious Conception ceremonies. Maintain the genealogical charts. Have mastered The Breath.
- **The Reverend Mother:** Solene. Has run the program for 25+ years. Claims to remember "the energetic imprint of every conception she's overseen." May actually believe her own mythology at this point — the line between manipulation and faith has blurred completely.

**The Prophecy:** Somewhere in the game's lore, scrawled in a journal or whispered by a burnt-out Say'yadina, is the Bene Tantra prophecy: *"When the breath of the masculine and the womb of the feminine align under the seventh moon of the sacred cycle, The One Who Breathes For All shall emerge — not born but manifested, not taught but remembered."* It sounds profound. It is, on close inspection, completely meaningless. Steve will point this out. Nobody will listen.

**The Darkest Joke:** The breeding program has been running for 30 years. It has produced zero enlightened beings. It has produced a LOT of confused children being raised in yurts. The Reverend Mother considers each failure "a lesson from the universe." Steve considers it "a 0% success rate, which in accounting is called fraud."

**The Mattis Connection:** Mattis's wife could be a low-level Bene Tantra initiate — or at least influenced by their philosophy. The "reparative intimacy" (lying still while apologizing for ancestral crimes) maps directly to Bene Tantra doctrine about "clearing masculine karma before conception." The spirit badger, the Buddha birth destiny — these are garbled versions of Bene Tantra teachings passed down through workshops to women who don't know the source. Mattis isn't being destroyed by his wife. He's being destroyed by an ideology she absorbed without knowing its origin. She thinks she invented this. She didn't.

**The Steve's Wife Connection:** The biggest reveal. Steve's wife Sarah wasn't lured by the Founder. The Founder was the lure — the bait retreat that the Bene Tantra use as a funnel. Sarah's "Ayahuasca retreat" that started everything? Run by a Say'yadina. The facilitator who "changed her life"? Bene Tantra. Every step of her journey from suburban wife to vanished hippie was choreographed. Not because Sarah is special — because her grandmother was a midwife in rural Vermont, and midwife bloodlines are "high-vibration" in Bene Tantra genealogy. Steve's tax-accountant wife is a target in a breeding program she doesn't know exists. Steve will find this out. It will make him very, very angry. And anger, in this game, is a resource.

**Tone Notes:** The satire here targets how systems of sexual control and reproductive ideology exist inside progressive/spiritual spaces — wrapped in consent language and feminist aesthetics but operating on the same ancient mechanics (controlling women's bodies, breeding programs, hierarchies of access). The Bene Tantra think they're dismantling the patriarchy. They've rebuilt it with better lighting. Reverend Mother Solene is not a cartoon villain — she's a true believer who has done real harm in the name of something she genuinely thinks is sacred. That makes her scarier than the Founder, who's just a grifter. The Dune references should be recognizable to anyone who's read the books but never explicitly named — the game's characters don't know they're in a Dune homage. Only the player does.
