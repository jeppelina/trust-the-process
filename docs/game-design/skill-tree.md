# Skill Tree & Daily Progression — "Trust the Process"

## THE CORE INSIGHT

Steve has two ways to fight: his body and his mouth. That's the player's choice — warrior or wizard. But there's a third thing happening that Steve can't control: the longer he stays at the retreat, the more hippie stuff seeps in. He doesn't choose it. He can't stop it. His Normality drops, and spiritual abilities just... appear.

Physical and Verbal are the **player's choice**. Spiritual is **the game's choice**. Everyone gets it. There is no build that avoids learning hippie stuff. That's the joke and the emotional engine: Steve is becoming the thing he came here to save his wife from, and the player can't prevent it — only decide whether to lean into it or try to ignore abilities they already have.

## TWO BRANCHES, ONE CURRENT

```
         PHYSICAL ◄─────────────────► VERBAL
         (Fists)        PLAYER         (Mouth)
            │          CHOOSES            │
            │                             │
            │    ┌─────────────────┐      │
            └───►│   SPIRITUAL     │◄─────┘
                 │  (Involuntary)  │
                 │                 │
                 │  Enhances both  │
                 │  branches.      │
                 │  Can't be       │
                 │  avoided.       │
                 └─────────────────┘
```

The player invests in Physical, Verbal, or both. Spiritual progression happens underneath — a rising tide that lifts (and transforms) everything Steve can do. As Normality drops, spiritual enhancements attach to existing Physical and Verbal skills, making them weirder and stronger. Steve's Slap becomes a Slap that channels energy. His Insult becomes an Insult that reads your aura first.

## BRANCH 1: PHYSICAL (Fists)

**The arc:** Steve has never been in a fight. He starts pathetic. Each level is him snapping a little harder — from embarrassing flailing to genuine, frightening competence. The comedy is the transformation. The tragedy is that he's becoming someone he wouldn't recognize.

**Damage type:** Physical. Simple, honest, undeniable. Hippies are not used to it.

## BRANCH 2: VERBAL (Mouth)

**The arc:** Steve starts with polite deflection and escalates to psychological warfare. This is the branch that gets dark. Steve discovers that words are weapons and that he's better with them than he expected. The progression mirrors how people actually escalate in conflict: boundaries → complaints → insults → cruelty → manipulation.

**Damage type:** Psychic. Emotional. The damage you can't see.

**Why "Mouth"?** "Talk" sounds passive. "Mouth" is a verb and a weapon. The battle menu button says **MOUTH**.

## THE SPIRITUAL CURRENT (Involuntary)

**This is not a branch the player chooses. It's what happens to Steve.**

Every day Steve spends at the retreat, his Normality drops. Sleeping in the dorms: -1. Attending a ceremony: -3. Ego death: -5. Talking to NPCs who believe: -1 here, -1 there. It's slow, it's invisible, and it's unstoppable. Steve can no more prevent it than you can prevent getting a tan in the sun.

As Normality drops past thresholds, two things happen:

1. **Standalone spiritual abilities unlock** — things Steve can use on their own (healing, defense, crowd control). These appear in the SPIRIT menu.
2. **Spiritual enhancements attach to existing Physical and Verbal skills** — making them weirder, stronger, and more uncomfortable for Steve. These replace or upgrade skills in the FIGHT and MOUTH menus automatically.

The player never "invests" in spiritual. They just wake up one morning and Steve can do things he couldn't do yesterday. The horror is gradual.

These are the abilities Steve can access directly. They're mostly defensive, healing, and utility — the spiritual path doesn't give Steve new ways to attack so much as new ways to survive, perceive, and endure.

| < 70 | **The Nod** | 2 | De-escalate one enemy for 1 turn + buff next attack +20% | The nod is involuntary now. He does it in his sleep. It works on everyone. |

### Spiritual Enhancements (Automatic Upgrades)

This is the key mechanic. As Normality drops, Steve's existing Physical and Verbal skills transform. The player doesn't choose this. They open their skill menu one morning and their Slap now says something different. The old version is gone.

**The comedy:** Steve is horrified. His normal-person attacks are becoming hippie powers. He's still punching people, but now his fist glows.

**The mechanics:** Enhanced skills keep their base damage type but add spiritual damage on top and gain bonus effects. They cost slightly more EP but are significantly stronger.

### The Horror of Enhancement

The key emotional beat: **Steve doesn't want these upgrades.** Each enhancement should trigger a one-time notification with Steve's reaction:

> **SKILL TRANSFORMED: Slap → Energy Slap**
> *Steve notices his hands tingling before he strikes. The impact leaves a faint shimmer. He shakes his hand out and mutters "static electricity" even though there's no carpet within a mile.*

> **SKILL TRANSFORMED: Insult → Accurate Insult**
> *Steve said something about the enemy's father. He was right. He doesn't know how he was right. He's never met their father. He needs to stop knowing things about strangers.*

> **SKILL TRANSFORMED: Punch → Chakra Strike**
> *Steve's knuckles hit the target and something pulsed. Not from Steve's fist — from inside the target. Steve felt it travel up his arm. He knows exactly where the weak point is now. He wants to throw up.*

The player can still see the original skill name crossed out: ~~Slap~~ → Energy Slap. A constant reminder of what Steve used to be.

## THE "ONE MORE DAY" LOOP

### Three Hooks Working Together

**Hook 1: The Skill Page (Choice)**

The ☰ menu's Skills tab shows the Physical and Verbal trees with visible progress. Locked skills show their requirement: "Punch — 15 physical attacks (you have 11)." The player can always see how close they are to the next unlock. This is the primary "one more day" driver.

**Hook 2: The Normality Ticker (Inevitability)**

A separate section of the Skills page shows Steve's Normality with upcoming spiritual thresholds: "Normality: 72. Next transformation at 70: The Nod + Crystal Launch." The player can see what's coming and can't stop it. Some players will deliberately lower Normality for the upgrades. Others will dread each transformation. Both reactions are correct.

**Hook 3: Daily Perk (Variety)**

Each morning Steve wakes up and is offered a **Daily Perk** — a small temporary bonus lasting until the next sleep. Pick 1 of 3 random options.

Examples:
- "Restless Night: +3 damage to all physical attacks today"
- "Overheard a Mantra: Spiritual abilities cost 1 less EP today"
- "Irritable: First verbal attack each fight does double damage"
- "Accidentally Meditated: Start each fight with +10 EP"
- "Bad Dreams: -10 max HP today but +30% psychic damage"
- "Suspiciously Well-Rested: +20 max HP today (Steve slept through a sound healing. He'll never admit it helped.)"
- "Deja Vu: One random defeated enemy stays dead today (doesn't respawn)"

**Hook 4: NPC Memory**

NPCs track Steve's behavior and comment on it:
- Heavy physical use → Frank: "You've got a good arm, kid. Remind me of myself when I still cared."
- Spiritual skills appearing → Brandon: "I KNEW you'd open up! We should do breathwork together!"
- Heavy verbal use → Receptionist: "Your energy feels... sharper today, Akash Prem."
- Low Normality → Everyone starts treating Steve like a member. He gets invited to things. It's awful.
- 3+ ego deaths → NPCs say "Welcome back, brother" with genuine warmth.

### Unlock Pacing

**Nothing important should unlock in the first 2 days.** The player should feel momentum without getting payoff yet.

```
DAY 1:  Steve has Slap, Complain, Set Boundary. He's pathetic.
        Fights Tier 0 enemies. Earns attack counters.
        Normality: 90 → ~85.

        End of day: ~5 physical, ~3 verbal.
        Skills tab: "Shove: 3/3 physical attacks ✓ — UNLOCKED TOMORROW"
        Spiritual: Panic Breathing appears (Normality < 85).
        That's it. One healing ability. Steve barely notices.

SLEEP → Daily perk choice. HP/EP restore. Enemies respawn.

DAY 2:  Shove unlocked. Maybe Nag.
        Handles Tier 0 more easily. Tier 1 enemies appear.
        Normality: ~82. Nothing new spiritually yet.

        End of day: ~12 physical, ~8 verbal.
        Throw Rock is close. Insult is close.
        ONE MORE FIGHT would do it... but Steve's tired...
        (this is the hook)

SLEEP → New daily perk.

DAY 3:  Throw Rock and Insult unlock.
        Steve feels meaningfully stronger.
        Normality: ~75. Energy Slap and Sage Toss appear.
        "Why is my slap glowing?" notification.
        First taste of involuntary spiritual growth.

        Boss gate opens. Steve could TRY but isn't ready.

DAY 4-5: Punch, Mock unlock.
         Normality: ~65. Force Push appears. Mantra Nag.
         More transformations. Steve is visibly disturbed.
         Boss fight becomes viable with evidence.

DAY 6+:  Higher tiers for completionists.
         Headbutt, Gaslight, Sound Bath.
         Normality: ~50. Multiple transformations stacking.
         Steve is a different person. The player notices.
```

### The "Almost" Principle

At the end of every day, at least one skill should be almost-but-not-quite unlocked. The math: if Tier 1 needs 3 attacks and Day 1 generates ~5, the player unlocks it overnight. Good — instant gratification. But Tier 2 needs 8. They have 5. They need 3 more. That's "one more day."

Normality thresholds should be similarly tuned: each day's natural drop should land Steve just ABOVE the next transformation threshold, so the player knows "tomorrow, something happens to my skills."

## WHAT RESETS AND WHAT DOESN'T

### Permanent (Never Resets)

- Attack counters (physical attacks used, verbal attacks used)
- Unlocked skills
- Spiritual enhancements (once transformed, stays transformed)
- Insight level
- Normality level (only goes DOWN, never back up — you can't un-learn this)
- Quest progress and evidence
- NPC relationship states
- Ego death count
- Inventory and equipment

### Resets on Sleep

- HP and EP (fully restored)
- Daily perk (new choice tomorrow)
- Minor enemy defeat flags (they respawn)
- Temporary buffs and debuffs
- Today's encounter history

### Resets on Ego Death

- Same as sleep, PLUS:
- Normality drops 5 (involuntary spiritual growth — dying opens you up)
- 50% of carried consumables lost
- The day advances (you lose time)

## DAMAGE TYPES

Three types, clean and simple:

| Type | Source | Strong Against | Weak Against |
|------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| **Physical** | Punching, throwing, tackling | Anyone with no combat training (most hippies) | Trained practitioners, bosses with shields |
| **Psychic** | Insults, guilt, manipulation, social damage | Anyone with insecurity or a persona to protect | True believers (immune to doubt), psychopaths |
| **Spiritual** | Crystals, breathwork, sound, energy, enhanced skills | Anyone spiritually "open" (everyone here except early-Steve) | Grounded people, high-Normality characters |

Enhanced skills deal their original type PLUS spiritual. This is why spiritual progression makes everything stronger — it adds a second damage type to attacks, hitting enemies on two fronts.

## BATTLE MENU

```
┌──────────────────────────────┐
│  FIGHT   MOUTH   SPIRIT     │
│  ITEM    ENDURE  FLEE       │
└──────────────────────────────┘
```

- **FIGHT** → All unlocked physical skills. Enhanced versions replace originals (shown as ~~Slap~~ Energy Slap).
- **MOUTH** → All unlocked verbal skills. Enhanced versions replace originals.
- **SPIRIT** → Standalone spiritual abilities only (Panic Breathing, Sound Bath, Hold Space, etc.). This menu starts empty and fills involuntarily. Early game it's greyed out with tooltip: "Steve doesn't do... whatever this would be."
- **ITEM** → Use a consumable.
- **ENDURE** → Defend (50% damage reduction). Some enemies lose interest if Steve endures 3 turns.
- **FLEE** → Always available. No shame.

**Analyze is gone** as a separate action. Cold Read (Verbal Tier 4) and Empathic Read (Spiritual, Normality < 45) serve this function. Early game, you fight blind. Late game, you see everything.

## THE EMOTIONAL ARC

The skill tree tells a story that the player doesn't fully control.

**What the player chooses:**

- **The Brawler** (Physical focus): Steve becomes someone who solves problems with force. NPCs fear him. Frank approves. But as Normality drops, his violence becomes spiritual violence. His punches glow. His tackles heal him. He's not just a fighter anymore — he's a warrior monk and he's horrified.

- **The Manipulator** (Verbal focus): Steve discovers he's terrifyingly good at reading and controlling people. He's becoming the thing he came here to rescue his wife from. But as Normality drops, his manipulation becomes empathic. His gaslighting uses their own spiritual language. His cold reads become aura reads. He's not just cruel anymore — he's a dark guru.

- **The Generalist** (Both branches): The most flexible builds, getting physical and verbal options but fewer high-tier skills in either. Their spiritual enhancements spread across both trees.

**What happens to everyone:**

Regardless of build, Steve's abilities transform. By endgame, EVERY version of Steve has become partially spiritual. The Brawler Steve does chakra strikes. The Manipulator Steve reads auras. There is no version of Steve that leaves the retreat unchanged.

**The wife, when Steve finally finds her, should react to what he's become.** If he's a Brawler, she's scared of his violence but fascinated by his energy. If he's a Manipulator, she recognizes the techniques — they're HER techniques. If he's balanced... she sees someone who went through the same journey she did, from the opposite direction.

That's the payoff for the whole system. The skill tree isn't just mechanics — it's Steve's character arc, happening in real time, partly chosen and partly forced.
