# Item & Loot System

## Item Categories

### 1. Weapons (What Steve Hits People With)

Steve doesn't carry swords. He carries objects that happen to be effective in this specific world.

**Weapon Slots:** Main Hand + Off-Hand (or two-handed)

**Weapon Types:**

| Type | Damage Type | Speed | Examples |
|------|------------|-------|---------|
| **Blunt Objects** | Physical | Medium | Singing bowl (blunt), crystal cluster (heavy), didgeridoo (two-handed) |
| **Throwables** | Physical / Spiritual | Fast | Rocks, crystals, sage bundles (smoke/confusion) |
| **Found Tools** | Physical | Slow but powerful | Groundskeeper's wrench, kitchen ladle, tent pole |
| **Psychological Weapons** | Psychic | Fast | Evidence documents, exposing questions, weaponized corporate speak |
| **Accidental Spiritual** | Spiritual | Varies | Items that channel spiritual energy despite Steve's denial |

Note: Steve's primary damage comes from his unlocked skills (FIGHT/MOUTH/SPIRIT), not from equipped weapons. Weapons in the prototype provide passive bonuses rather than replacing the skill system.

**Weapon Scaling:** Weapons don't level up in a traditional sense. Instead, Steve finds contextually more powerful items as zones escalate. A singing bowl is devastating at a retreat center; at Burning Man you might need a megaphone or a fire poi.

### 2. Armor (What Steve Wears)

Steve doesn't wear armor. He wears clothes. Some clothes happen to protect him in this world.

**Armor Slots:** Body, Legs, Feet, Head

| Slot | Normality-Preserving | Normality-Reducing | Notes |
|------|---------------------|-------------------|-------|
| **Body** | Polo shirt, button-down, suit jacket | Sanctuary hoodie, hemp poncho, tie-dye | What Steve wears signals which world he belongs to |
| **Legs** | Khakis, chinos, slacks | Yoga pants, harem pants, sarong | Yoga pants genuinely give agility bonuses. Steve hates this. |
| **Feet** | Sensible shoes, loafers | Barefoot, Birkenstocks, toe shoes | Barefoot is required in some areas. Steve suffers. |
| **Head** | Nothing (Steve doesn't wear hats) | Flower crown, turban wrap, headband | Head items are mostly spiritual-world items |

**Armor Philosophy:** There's no "best" armor. Corporate wear protects Normality and boosts Psychic damage (Steve's corporate confidence is weaponized) but makes spiritual NPCs dismiss you. Spiritual wear lowers Normality but opens doors and social options. The player has to dress Steve for the situation, which is its own comedy.

### 3. Accessories

**Accessory Slots:** 2 slots. Mix and match.

| Accessory | Effect | Comedy Value |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Consent Card | Blocks 1 physical attack per battle | Steve laminating a consent card is peak Steve |
| Mala Beads | +2 Insight gain per encounter | Steve fidgets with them like a stress toy |
| Wi-Fi Password | Access to internet-based quest actions | Most powerful item at any retreat |
| Business Card Holder | +3 Credibility with normal people | Absolutely useless in spiritual settings |
| Crystal Pouch | +2 all spiritual stats | Steve carries it "ironically" (he's not sure anymore) |
| Fitbit | +2 Awareness (step tracking = map awareness) | Steve checks his step count during ceremonies |
| Pocket Calculator | +5 Psychic damage | The sound of the buttons infuriates spiritual enemies |
| Wedding Ring | +5 HP, passive reminder of why he's here | Removing it unlocks certain dialogue but reduces max HP |

### 5. Quest Items (Key Items)

Non-consumable, non-equippable items that advance the story or unlock content.

- **Wife's Credit Card Receipt** — Starting item. What led Steve here.
- **Recommendation Letters** — One per zone. Traces the wife's path.
- **Evidence Documents** — Financial records, labor violations, etc. Used in boss fights.
- **Access Keys/Passes** — Unlock restricted areas within zones.
- **The Wife's Journal** (fragments found across zones) — Pieces of her perspective. Humanizes her journey. Each entry is from a different phase of her transformation. Steve reads them and they hurt in different ways.

### 6. Joke Items (Flavor / Achievement)

Items with no mechanical purpose except being funny. Collecting them all could unlock something.

- **Bumper Sticker Collection** — One per zone. Each more unhinged than the last.
- **Business Cards** — From every self-proclaimed healer/coach/guru Steve meets. A growing stack.
- **Steve's Voicemails** — His boss keeps calling. Each message is more confused/angry. Playable audio items.
- **Photos of Steve Looking Uncomfortable** — Automatically collected at key moments. Gallery unlockable.

## Loot System

### Drop Rules

- Every enemy drops at least 1 item + Cacao Coins
- Drop tables have weighted rarity: Common (60%), Uncommon (25%), Rare (10%), Legendary (5%)
- Some drops are enemy-specific (Consent Card only from Boundary Violator)
- Boss drops are guaranteed unique items
- Side quest rewards are always better than combat drops (incentivize non-violence)

### Rarity Tiers

| Tier | Color | Description | Examples |
|------|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Common** | White | Basic items found everywhere | Incense, kombucha, generic crystals |
| **Uncommon** | Green | Better items, zone-specific | Branded merch, specific oils, workshop handouts |
| **Rare** | Blue | Powerful items, limited availability | Actual coffee, Wi-Fi passwords, evidence documents |
| **Legendary** | Purple | One-of-a-kind items, often from bosses | The Founder's robe, ceremonial items, Steve's wedding ring upgrade |
| **Cursed** | Red | Powerful but with significant downsides | Guru's amulet (+20 Charisma, -30 Normality, NPCs start calling you "teacher") |

### Economy

**Primary Currency: Cacao Coins**
- Earned from combat, quests, selling items
- Accepted at gift shops, market stalls, and donation-based-but-actually-mandatory exchanges
- Inflation across zones: things cost more at Burning Man than at the yoga studio (realistic)

**Secondary Currency: Gratitude Tokens**
- Earned from helping NPCs, non-combat resolutions, side quests
- Used for exclusive items and unlocks that money can't buy
- The irony: the gift economy actually works but only for Steve because he doesn't believe in it

**Tertiary Currency: Actual Money (Steve's Salary)**
- Steve has a real bank account. He can spend real money.
- Absurdly powerful in early zones (he can just... buy things at normal prices)
- Less effective in later zones where the economy is weirder
- Using real money lowers Vibe ("he's so ATTACHED to the material")
- But sometimes the most practical solution is just... paying for things. Like a normal person.

## Inventory Management

### Capacity
- **Main Inventory:** 20 slots (Steve's messenger bag from work)
- **Upgrade:** Backpack from gift shop = 30 slots. Festival fanny pack = 25 slots but +2 Agility.
- **Key Items:** Separate tab, unlimited space
- **Equipment:** Separate tab (what's currently worn/wielded)

### Item Descriptions
Every item has TWO descriptions:
1. **Stat description:** Mechanical info (damage, healing, buffs)
2. **Steve's description:** His internal monologue about the item. This is where a lot of the humor lives.

Example:
> **Moldavite Pendant**
> Stats: +3 Spiritual damage, +2 Charisma, -2 Normality
> Steve: *"$400 for a green rock from the Czech Republic. The seller said it was formed by a meteorite impact 15 million years ago. That part might actually be true. The part about it 'accelerating my soul's evolution' — less certain."*
