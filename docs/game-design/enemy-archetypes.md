# Enemy Archetypes & Type System

## Damage Types (3-Type System)

| Type | Source | Strong Against | Weak Against |
|------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| **Physical** | Punching, throwing, tackling | Anyone with no combat training (most hippies) | Trained practitioners, bosses with shields |
| **Psychic** | Insults, guilt, manipulation, complaints | Anyone with insecurity or a persona to protect | True believers (immune to doubt), psychopaths |
| **Spiritual** | Crystals, breathwork, sound, energy, enhanced skills | Anyone spiritually "open" (everyone here except early-Steve) | Grounded people, high-Normality characters |

Enhanced skills deal their original type PLUS spiritual, hitting enemies on two fronts.

## Enemy Types (Classes)

Every enemy belongs to a TYPE that determines their damage profile, weaknesses, and behavior patterns. Types persist across zones — you'll encounter variations of these archetypes at every stop on the spiritual pipeline.

| Type | Primary Damage | Weakness | Behavior |
|------|---------------|----------|----------|
| **MLM Zealot** | Psychic + gold drain | Psychic (they're insecure underneath) | Pushy. Won't fight but won't leave. Escalates sales pitch each turn. |
| **Boundary Crosser** | Physical + Psychic | Physical (not used to being hit back) | Approaches and touches without consent. Stuns through contact. |
| **True Believer** | Spiritual + Psychic | Physical (they've never experienced real conflict) | Genuinely believes. Hard to fight because they're not malicious. |
| **Status Climber** | Psychic (ego attacks) | Psychic (fragile ego under the flex) | Competitive. Everything is about hierarchy. Attacks are flexes. |
| **Wounded Healer** | Psychic (guilt + manipulation) | Psychic (genuine compassion breaks them) | Uses pain as a weapon. Defeating them feels bad. |
| **Gatekeeper** | Psychic (authority, denial) | Psychic (expose their real backstory) | Blocks progress. Must be outsmarted or undermined. |
| **Facilitator** | Psychic + Spiritual | Psychic (catch them being fake) | Trained in deflection. Must be exposed with evidence or verbal attacks. |
| **Karma Worker** | Psychic (guilt) | Psychic (kindness converts them to ally) | Tragic enemy. Low HP, low damage. Not fighting them is usually better. |
| **Guru/Boss** | All types | Psychic (especially deep personal truths) | Multi-phase. Each phase strips a layer of persona. |

## Type Interactions (Simplified Matrix)

```
                    TARGET TYPE
                    MLM    Boundary  True     Status   Wounded  Gate    Facil.  Karma
ATTACK TYPE         Zealot Crosser   Believer Climber  Healer   keeper  itator  Worker
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Physical            1x     1.5x      1.5x     1x       1.5x     0.5x    1x      1.5x
Psychic             1.5x   1x        0.5x     1.5x     1.5x     1.5x    1.5x    1.5x
Spiritual           0.5x   1x        0.5x     1x       1x       0.5x    0.5x    1x
```

Key takeaways:
- **Physical works on the unprepared.** True Believers, Boundary Crossers, and Karma Workers have never been hit. They don't know what to do.
- **Psychic beats insecurity.** MLM Zealots, Status Climbers, Wounded Healers, and Gatekeepers all have something to hide.
- **Spiritual is unreliable** against most enemies — they're already steeped in spiritual energy. It's strong as a BONUS type on enhanced skills, not as a primary weapon.
- **Verbal attacks (Mouth skills) deal psychic damage**, making the verbal branch effective against the broadest range of enemies.
- **Physical branch is devastating against select targets** but less effective against social/authority types like Gatekeepers.
- **The hybrid strategy (both branches) gives the most flexibility**, which is the intended sweet spot.

## Zone 1 Enemies (The Sanctuary)

### Essential Oil Warrior (MLM Zealot)
- **HP:** 30 | **ATK:** 8 | **DEF:** 0
- **Weakness:** Psychic
- **Attacks:** Anoint (10 dmg, peppermint oil), MLM Pitch (7 dmg, psychic)
- **Behavior:** Pushy. Ambushes Steve on the path. Every dialogue choice leads to combat.
- **Respawns daily.** Good for grinding physical/verbal counters.

### Competitive Meditator (True Believer)
- **HP:** 45 | **ATK:** 10 | **DEF:** 2
- **Weakness:** Physical
- **Attacks:** Judgmental Silence (9 dmg), Aggressive Namaste (13 dmg)
- **Behavior:** Territorial. Challenges Steve's technique. Has never been physically hit.
- **Respawns daily.** Tougher than Oil Warrior, better Insight reward.

### The Gatekeeper (Gatekeeper)
- **HP:** 60 | **ATK:** 14 | **DEF:** 3
- **Weakness:** Psychic
- **Attacks:** Gatekeep (15 dmg), Spiritual Authority (11 dmg)
- **Behavior:** Blocks access to the ceremony space/back office. Real name is Dave Carver. Former corporate middle-manager with a shame response.
- **Does not respawn** once defeated.

### The Founder — Paul Fontaine (Boss)
- **Phase 1 — Brandon (Brainwashed):** 30 HP shield. Eager Defense (8 dmg), Gratitude Shield (defend), Love Bomb (6 dmg). Must break Brandon's conditioning first.
- **Phase 2 — Paul:** 50 HP. Charismatic Redirect (14 dmg), Gaslighting (10 dmg + smudge), Financial Restructure (buff self), Inner Circle Invitation (18 dmg).
- **Phase 3 — Desperate Paul:** At ≤15 HP. Desperate Plea (6 dmg), Full Meltdown (22 dmg), Vulnerable Honesty (0 dmg — he just... tells the truth).
- **Weakness:** Psychic (personal truths, exposure of his real identity)
- **Resolution:** Three endings — walk away with documents, restructure the Sanctuary, send everything to authorities.

## Boss Design Pattern

Every zone boss follows a 3-phase structure:

### Phase 1: The Mask
- An ally or shield defends the boss
- Separate HP pool — must be broken through first
- Talk/verbal skills are especially effective here

### Phase 2: The Crack
- Mask slips. The real person emerges, angry and defensive.
- Standard combat with boss-specific mechanics.
- Boss uses weaponized versions of their spiritual practice.

### Phase 3: The Person
- The human underneath. Scared, sad, or lost.
- Boss becomes erratic — mix of genuine vulnerability and last-ditch manipulation.
- Player chooses resolution: destroy, leave, or something creative.
- Resolution choice affects zone epilogue and potentially future zones.

### The Emotional Rule
Every boss should have a moment where the player thinks: "Oh. They're just a person." Not to excuse their behavior — but to complicate the victory.
