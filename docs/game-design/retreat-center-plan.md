# THE SANCTUARY — Full Zone Plan

## OVERVIEW

The Sanctuary is the first complete zone of "Trust the Process." Steve Stevens arrives looking for his wife, who was last seen here before disappearing deeper into the spiritual pipeline. What begins as a simple "ask where she went" turns into a 45-60 minute journey through escalating absurdity, as The Sanctuary reveals itself to be a well-oiled machine for separating people from their money and autonomy.

**Core dramatic question:** Where did Steve's wife go, and why does nobody want to tell him?

**Core comedic engine:** Steve's aggressive normality is both his greatest weakness (he doesn't understand anything here) and his greatest weapon (his accounting skills are devastating against systems built on financial opacity).

---

## THE PLOT — CAUSE AND EFFECT CHAIN

The plot follows strict "this happens, THEREFORE that happens" logic. Nothing is random.

### ACT 1: ARRIVAL (Rooms 1-3)

**Beat 1: Check-In**
Steve arrives at THE WELCOME PAVILION. The Receptionist is friendly but takes his phone ("digital detox policy"). She assigns him a spiritual name: "Akash Prem" (Sky Love). Steve asks about his wife. The Receptionist says she "moved on to deeper work" and deflects further questions.

→ THEREFORE Steve has no phone and no direct answers. He must explore.

**Beat 2: The Garden Path Ambush**
Walking toward the garden, Steve is ambushed by the Essential Oil Warrior, who insists his "aura is critically depleted" and tries to sell him a $200 oil package. Dialogue options fail to defuse her.

→ THEREFORE Steve fights his first combat encounter. He wins (she's not hard), learns the combat system, and notices she drops a RETREAT MAP showing rooms he hasn't seen yet. This opens navigation to new areas.

**Beat 3: The Only Sane Man**
In THE SACRED GARDEN, Steve meets Frank the Groundskeeper. Frank is the first person who talks normally. He confirms Steve's wife WAS here, she was "a good one — actually helped fix things instead of just meditating about fixing things." She got noticed by The Founder, invited to "inner circle work," then left with a recommendation letter for somewhere else. Frank doesn't know where. But he knows The Founder keeps records in the Back Office.

→ THEREFORE Steve now has a concrete goal: get into the Back Office to find where his wife went. But the Back Office is locked.

### ACT 2: INVESTIGATION (Rooms 4-7)

**Beat 4: The Kitchen**
Steve visits THE KITCHEN looking for the Back Office key. He meets Maya, a Karma Yogi who's been "volunteering" for 7 months on what was supposed to be a 2-week stay. She's skeptical but trapped. She knew Steve's wife — they worked kitchen shifts together. Maya says the wife started asking uncomfortable questions about the finances, and that's when The Founder got "interested" in her. Maya has information but won't talk here — too many ears. She says to meet her at THE FIRE PIT after the evening program.

→ THEREFORE Steve needs to survive until evening. He has to attend the evening program (the Sharing Circle) to get to the fire pit afterward.

**Beat 5: The Dorms**
Steve is assigned a bed in THE DORMS. His bunkmate is Brandon, a fresh arrival who's enthusiastically drinking the Kool-Aid. Brandon thinks everything is amazing. Steve notices Brandon's expensive watch is gone — "Oh, I donated it to the Gratitude Fund. Material attachments, you know?" Brandon asks Steve to come to yoga.

→ THEREFORE Brandon establishes the social pressure mechanic. Also, Steve finds a VOLUNTEER SCHEDULE tacked to the dorm wall showing that Karma Yogis work 14-hour days. This is evidence for later.

**Beat 6: The Movement Studio**
Steve can optionally visit THE MOVEMENT STUDIO. If he goes, he encounters The Competitive Meditator, who challenges Steve to a meditation duel ("I can tell your practice is... nascent"). This is a MINOR FIGHT where the Meditator attacks with psychic damage and Steve must either endure or use Talk actions to deflect.

Win: The Meditator grudgingly respects Steve and mentions that "the real stuff happens in the Ceremony Space, but you need The Gatekeeper's approval." This gives Steve intel about another path to the Back Office (through the Ceremony Space, which is adjacent).

→ THEREFORE Steve learns there are TWO routes to the Back Office.

**Beat 7: The Sharing Circle (Required)**
THE MAIN HALL hosts the evening Sharing Circle. This is a multi-round endurance encounter — not a fight, but a survival challenge. Steve sits in a circle. NPCs share increasingly personal/absurd things. Steve must respond to 3 rounds:

- Round 1: The Oversharer trauma-dumps. Steve can Validate, Deflect, or Endure. Wrong answer → Oversharer follows Steve around later.
- Round 2: The facilitator asks Steve to share. Steve can give a corporate answer ("I'm here for professional development"), share honestly ("My wife left"), or refuse. Honest answer → +Insight, others relate. Corporate answer → laughs (from player, not NPCs). Refuse → suspicious looks.
- Round 3: The circle does a "group exercise" that's essentially synchronized moaning. Steve can join (humiliating but +Acceptance) or refuse (maintains Normality but people notice).

This is NOT combat — it's a dialogue/choice gauntlet. But wrong choices have consequences.

→ THEREFORE the Sharing Circle is over and Steve can now go to the Fire Pit. Also, his choices here affect how NPCs treat him in Act 3.

### ACT 3: ESCALATION (Rooms 8-10)

**Beat 8: The Fire Pit (Key Scene)**
After the Sharing Circle, Steve goes to THE FIRE PIT. Maya is there. In the firelight, she tells Steve the full story:

- His wife arrived 3 months ago. Normal at first.
- She started helping in the kitchen, then got interested in "the work."
- She asked Maya: "Do you ever feel like this place runs on guilt?"
- The Founder noticed her — he always notices the smart ones.
- She was invited to private sessions, then the inner circle.
- One day she left with a recommendation letter. Maya heard her say she was going to "a breathwork intensive upstate."
- The recommendation letter is in the Back Office filing cabinet, along with all the financial records The Founder doesn't want people to see.

Maya also reveals: she wants to leave but The Founder has her passport (as "safekeeping"). She asks Steve to grab her documents from the Back Office too.

→ THEREFORE Steve now has FULL MOTIVATION and a SIDE QUEST. He needs to break into the Back Office. Maya gives him a TIP: the Ceremony Space has a connecting door to the Back Office. The Gatekeeper guards the Ceremony Space, but he goes to sleep after the 10pm meditation.

**Beat 9: The Night Infiltration**
Steve must navigate THE SANCTUARY AT NIGHT. The grounds are different — new ambient text, some NPCs are gone, others appear:

- Brandon is in the garden, staring at the moon, talking about "feeling his edges dissolve." He's deeper in. He calls Steve "brother" now.
- A pair of Karma Yogis are whispering about how they haven't been paid in months.
- The Cacao Dealer appears near the kitchen, offering "ceremonial grade" drinks. He's a BLACK MARKET merchant — sells actual useful items (coffee, phone charger, bus schedule) for cacao coins. He's the shop.

Steve must get past THE GATEKEEPER to reach the Ceremony Space. Three options:

**Path A — Sneak (requires: won meditation duel OR found Volunteer Schedule)**
Wait until 10pm. The Gatekeeper falls asleep after meditation. Steve sneaks past. If caught → forced dialogue → possible fight.

**Path B — Confront (requires: Insight ≥ 5)**
Steve can challenge The Gatekeeper's authority directly. Using Risk Assessment reveals the Gatekeeper (Dharma John) worked at Verizon 18 months ago and got divorced. He's not ancient wisdom — he's a recently divorced telecom middle manager. Exposing this lets Steve through.

**Path C — Frank's Key (requires: completed Frank's side quest)**
If Steve helped Frank fix the burst pipe in the garden (available anytime in Act 2), Frank gives him a MASTER KEY that opens the Back Office directly, bypassing the Ceremony Space entirely. This is the reward for being a decent person.

**Beat 10: The Back Office**
Steve enters THE BACK OFFICE (through whichever path). It's a normal office — Dell laptop, filing cabinet, printer, motivational poster that says "Synergy." This is hilarious because it looks exactly like Steve's office at work.

Steve finds:
- **The recommendation letter** for his wife → She went to "Pranayama Peak Breathwork Intensive" (Zone 2 hook)
- **Financial records** → $1.2M annual revenue, 40 unpaid volunteers, $180K to The Founder's personal LLC
- **Maya's passport and documents** (if side quest active)
- **A folder labeled "PROSPECT LIST"** — every guest ranked by estimated net worth. Steve's wife was flagged as "HIGH VALUE — husband is accountant, potential donor"

As Steve reads the financial records, the lights turn on. The Founder is standing in the doorway.

"I thought you might end up here. The practical ones always do."

→ THEREFORE: Boss battle.

### ACT 4: CONFRONTATION (Boss Fight)

**The Founder Battle — Three Phases**

**Pre-battle dialogue:** The Founder is calm, reasonable, even charming. He admits the finances look bad "out of context" but frames everything as sacred work. He offers Steve a deal: take the letter, go find his wife, forget what he saw. Steve can accept (early exit, no boss fight, but haunted by guilt) or refuse.

If Steve refuses:

**PHASE 1: "I Don't Do Conflict" (HP 100%→60%)**
- The Founder doesn't attack directly. He SUMMONS FOLLOWERS — Brandon appears, fully converted, plus a Karma Yogi.
- Steve must fight through the followers. The Founder uses REFRAME (heals followers by "recontextualizing" their damage as growth).
- Key mechanic: Steve can use TALK → "Brandon, where's your watch?" to snap Brandon out of it. This removes the follower without fighting.
- Breaking the phase: Use SPREADSHEET ANALYSIS on The Founder while followers are down → reveals the financial records Steve just found → followers see the truth → followers leave.

**PHASE 2: "You Don't Understand What We're Building" (HP 60%→25%)**
- The Founder drops the calm act. He's angry, defensive, but still manipulative.
- Attacks: SPIRITUAL GASLIGHTING (confusion, Steve might attack himself), SACRED RAGE (physical — he throws a singing bowl), GUILT BOMB ("If you destroy this, where do these people GO?")
- The Guilt Bomb is the hardest attack — it's actually a fair point. Reduces Steve's attack power temporarily.
- Key mechanic: Use AUDIT to dismantle his arguments one by one. Each successful Audit reduces his defense.
- REFRAME still works but weaker. He's losing control of the narrative.

**PHASE 3: "You're Just Like Her" (HP 25%→0)**
- The Founder crumbles. He's not a villain — he's a scared man who built something bigger than he can handle.
- "Your wife said the same things. That's why I sent her away. She was going to ruin everything."
- He reveals: He didn't just give her a recommendation. He gave her a GLOWING recommendation specifically to get her away from his operation before she exposed him. She was the real threat, not Steve.
- Attacks are erratic — EXISTENTIAL CRISIS (hurts himself), DESPERATION GRAB (tries to take evidence).
- Steve can end this three ways:
  - **FULL AUDIT** (destroy) — Expose everything. The Sanctuary closes. People are displaced but free.
  - **WALK AWAY** (pragmatic) — Take the letter, leave the records. The Sanctuary continues. Steve got what he came for.
  - **RESTRUCTURE** (funniest) — Steve, the accountant, takes over the books. Sets up a proper LLC, payroll for volunteers, transparent pricing. The Sanctuary survives as a legitimate business. The Founder is demoted to "Community Coordinator."

### ACT 5: DEPARTURE

**The Morning After**

Depending on the ending choice, different departure scenes:

**Restructure ending** (canon funny ending):
The next morning. Steve has set up a SPREADSHEET projected on the Main Hall wall. Karma Yogis are getting contracts. The Receptionist is processing actual payroll. Frank is smiling. The Founder is sitting in a plastic chair labeled "COMMUNITY COORDINATOR" looking shell-shocked.

Steve gets his phone back (847 unread emails). He has the recommendation letter.

He walks to the Sanctuary gate. Maya is there with her passport, waiting for a bus.

MAYA: "You're really going after her?"
STEVE: "She's my wife."
MAYA: "She's not the same person you married."
STEVE: "Neither am I. I just restructured a wellness center. That's not in my job description."

Steve leaves. Text on screen:

**"Steve got his phone back. He had 847 unread emails, 3 voicemails from his mother, and a text from an unknown number.**

**The text said: 'I heard what you did at The Sanctuary. Don't come to Pranayama Peak. But also... hurry.'"**

**ZONE COMPLETE — THE SANCTUARY**

## COMBAT DESIGN

### Tutorial Fight: Essential Oil Warrior
- Purpose: Teach basic combat (Attack, Talk, Item)
- 80 HP. Attacks: Anoint (10 dmg + Smudged), MLM Pitch (escalates)
- Weakness: Any Talk action reveals she's losing money on the business
- 2-3 rounds. Player cannot lose unless they try.

### Optional Fight: Competitive Meditator
- Purpose: Teach Analyze and endurance mechanics
- 100 HP. Attacks: Performative Stillness (+DEF each turn), Humble Brag Sutra (psychic 15), Spiritual One-Upmanship (debuff)
- Weakness: Being Ignored (Steve's Bore talk action drains 20 HP/turn)
- Strategy: Don't attack while he's meditating (reflects). Wait for him to brag, then Bore him.

### Optional Fight: The Oversharer
- Purpose: Teach Talk > Validate mechanic
- 50 HP (fragile). Attacks: Trauma Dump (psychic 20 + DOT), "Can I Share Something?" (stun 1 turn), Ugly Cry (AOE — only relevant if companions later)
- Weakness: Validate (heals both, but de-escalates — 3 Validates ends fight peacefully)
- Can also just be punched. But the game makes you feel bad about it.

### Conditional Fight: The Gatekeeper
- Purpose: Test if player has gathered enough intel
- 150 HP. Attacks: "You're Not Ready" (blocks all attacks 1 turn), Spiritual Gaslighting (confusion), Ancient Wisdom (psychic 25)
- Weakness: Risk Assessment reveals his Verizon past. Each revealed truth removes 50 HP worth of "spiritual armor."
- Can be bypassed entirely with sneak or key path.

### Boss Fight: The Founder
See Act 4 above for full three-phase design.

Summary:
- Phase 1 (100→60 HP): Summons followers, uses Reframe to heal them. Break with Spreadsheet Analysis + evidence. Or talk Brandon out of it.
- Phase 2 (60→25 HP): Direct combat, Gaslighting/Sacred Rage/Guilt Bomb. Use Audit repeatedly.
- Phase 3 (25→0 HP): Erratic, vulnerable. Choose ending: Destroy / Walk Away / Restructure.

Total boss HP: effective ~200 with follower phase.

## DEATH MECHANIC

**Steve doesn't die. He "Achieves Ego Death."**

When HP hits 0:

The screen goes white. Ethereal music. A voice says: "Let go, Steve. Let go of your attachments. Let go of your spreadsheets. Let go of your perfectly organized desktop icons."

Steve wakes up in THE DORMS. A Karma Yogi is standing over him with a singing bowl.

"Welcome back, brother. You passed out during breathwork. Don't worry — ego death is a normal part of the process."

**Mechanical effects:**
- Steve respawns in the Dorms with 50% HP
- He keeps all items and quest progress
- He loses 5 Normality (getting more "open") — this is funny because dying makes Steve MORE spiritual against his will
- Any fight he was in resets
- NPCs have new dialogue acknowledging "your experience" with condescending approval

**Flavor text options (random each death):**
- "You had what the community calls 'a breakthrough.' You call it 'losing consciousness.'"
- "Steve lay on the floor of [location]. A circle of concerned faces looked down at him. Someone was already burning sage."
- "As Steve's vision faded, he heard someone whisper 'Should we call 911?' followed by 'No, this is sacred. Get the essential oils.'"
- "Steve respawned. He hated that he thought the word 'respawned.'"

## FRANK'S SIDE QUEST: "The Pipe That Binds"

**Trigger:** Talk to Frank a second time (frank_return). He mentions the irrigation pipe in the garden burst and nobody will help because "manual labor isn't spiritual enough."

**Steps:**
1. Frank gives Steve a WRENCH
2. Steve goes to the garden and interacts with a new clickable: "Burst Pipe"
3. Mini-puzzle: choose between three pipe connections (only one makes sense — the one that's practical, not the one labeled "sacred flow" or "intention pipe")
4. Pipe fixed. Frank is impressed. "You're the first person here who knows which end of a wrench to hold."
5. Frank gives Steve the MASTER KEY

**Comedy:** The pipe connections are labeled with spiritual names. The correct one is just labeled "WATER."

## SECRETS & OPTIONAL DISCOVERIES

1. **The Founder's Cabin** — If Steve finds the WiFi password (from Cacao Dealer for 100 cacao), he can use it in the Dorms to check his email. He has 847 emails. One is from his wife's email address, sent 2 months ago: "I'm sorry. I'm figuring something out. Don't worry about me." Reading this gives +3 Insight and changes the boss fight dialogue (The Founder doesn't know Steve has this).

2. **Brandon's Watch** — If Steve talks to Brandon at night and mentions the watch, Brandon briefly snaps out of it: "I... it was a graduation gift from my dad." Then snaps back: "But material things are just ego!" This is setup for the boss fight where Steve can invoke the watch to break Brandon's follower state.

3. **The Founder's Book** — Available at the gift shop for 150 cacao. "FINDING YOUR ENOUGH: A Journey Beyond Journey" by "Derek (The Founder)." Every quote is misattributed. Reading it gives +2 Insight and a devastating dialogue option in the boss fight.

4. **The Actual Financial Records** — Finding these in the Back Office isn't just a plot item. If Steve has high enough Insight (≥8), he notices an additional detail: The Founder has been sending monthly checks to a woman named "L. Stevens" at an address in upstate New York. His wife has been receiving money from The Founder. This doesn't resolve here — it's a Zone 2 mystery seed.
