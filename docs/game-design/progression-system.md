# Character Progression System

## Insight Levels

Insight is the game's XP/leveling system. It represents how much spiritual jargon and social fluency Steve has absorbed — NOT actual wisdom.

### Level Thresholds

| Level | Title | What Unlocks |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 0 | **The Uninitiated** | Starting state. NPCs treat Steve like a lost tourist. |
| 1 | **Mildly Curious** | Basic dialogue options. Can attend beginner workshops. People stop asking "is this your first time?" |
| 2 | **Cautiously Open** | Mid-tier dialogue. Can use spiritual jargon (badly). Some NPCs share information. |
| 3 | **Reluctantly Aware** | Advanced dialogue. Access to intermediate areas. Steve accidentally says "holding space" in conversation and is horrified. |
| 4 | **Uncomfortably Fluent** | Full dialogue access. Can bullshit convincingly. Enemies sometimes mistake Steve for a facilitator. |
| 5 | **Accidentally Awakened** | Master level. Steve can navigate any spiritual social situation. He hates that he can do this. Unlocks hidden dialogue, secret areas, and the game's best jokes. |

### How Insight Is Gained

| Source | Insight Gain | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Defeating enemies | +1 to +3 | Based on enemy difficulty |
| Talking down enemies | +2 to +5 | Non-combat resolution always gives more |
| Completing quests | +5 to +10 | Main quests give the most |
| Surviving workshops/ceremonies | +2 to +4 | Endurance-based encounters |
| Finding wife's journal entries | +3 | Also advances the emotional story |
| Accidentally having a genuine moment | +5 | Rare scripted events where Steve feels something real, panics, and gains Insight from the panic |
| Reading The Founder's book | +1 | But Steve loses 30 minutes of his life he'll never get back |

### Insight Level Effects on Gameplay

**Combat:** Higher Insight unlocks better Talk actions. At Level 3+, Steve can preemptively defuse some encounters before they become fights.

**Exploration:** Locked areas have Insight requirements. The Ceremony Space needs Level 3. Burning Man's inner playa needs Level 5.

**Dialogue:** Each Insight level unlocks new dialogue trees. At Level 0, Steve can only say normal things. At Level 5, he can match any NPC's spiritual jargon — but his delivery is always slightly off, which is funnier.

**Social:** NPCs react differently based on Insight. A Level 0 Steve gets dismissed. A Level 3 Steve gets included. A Level 5 Steve gets asked to lead a workshop (terrifying).

## Skill Trees

Steve has TWO skill trees that grow in opposition. Investing in one makes the other slightly weaker. The player can't max both.

### Tree 1: The Corporate Branch (Bureaucratic Skills)

These are Steve's accounting and corporate abilities, weaponized.

**Tier 1 (Available from start):**
| Skill | EP Cost | Effect |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **Spreadsheet Analysis** | 5 EP | Analyze one enemy: reveals stats, weaknesses, financial exposure. Some enemies are instantly defeated if their finances are bad enough. |
| **Schedule Optimization** | 4 EP | Grants Steve an extra action this turn. "I've blocked out 15 minutes for this conflict." |
| **Risk Assessment** | 3 EP | Reveals enemy backstory and hidden information. Also detects traps and scams. |

**Tier 2 (Insight Level 2+):**
| Skill | EP Cost | Effect |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **Audit** | 8 EP | Heavy Bureaucratic damage + Audited debuff (defense drops to 0). Devastating finisher. |
| **Tax Write-Off** | 4 EP | Reduce damage from next attack by 50%. "It's a business expense." |
| **Depreciation** | 6 EP | Apply damage-over-time to one enemy. Their value decreases each turn. |
| **Performance Review** | 5 EP | Psychic damage + chance of Existential Crisis debuff. Steve gives the enemy a formal evaluation. |

**Tier 3 (Insight Level 4+):**
| Skill | EP Cost | Effect |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **Hostile Takeover** | 12 EP | Turns one non-boss enemy into a temporary ally. Steve restructures their organization. |
| **Compliance Violation** | 10 EP | AOE Bureaucratic damage to all enemies. Steve reads aloud from the relevant regulatory code. |
| **Billable Hours** | Passive | Steve's damage increases by 5% for every turn the battle continues. He's on the clock. |
| **Forensic Accounting** | 15 EP | Ultimate ability. Full financial investigation. Against bosses, triggers phase transitions. Against normal enemies, instant defeat + bonus loot (you find where they hid the money). |

### Tree 2: The Reluctant Spiritual Branch

Skills Steve picks up ACCIDENTALLY by being immersed in this world. Each one comes with a story of how he didn't mean to learn it.

**Tier 1 (Insight Level 1+, Normality must be below 80%):**
| Skill | EP Cost | Effect | How Steve Learned It |
|-------|---------|--------|---------------------|
| **Accidental Breathwork** | 3 EP | Self-heal 15 HP. Steve hyperventilates during a panic attack and it... works? | Had a panic attack during a breathwork session. The facilitator congratulated him. |
| **Corporate Meditation** | 4 EP | +20% defense for 3 turns. Focus and calm from a workplace wellness program. | His company's mandatory mindfulness module. 10 minutes on a Tuesday. He hated it. But it stuck. |
| **Ironic Crystal** | 3 EP | Throwable. 15 Spiritual damage. Someone gave Steve a crystal "as a joke" and it inexplicably causes real damage. | A Karma Yogi pressed it into his hand and said "you need this." |

**Tier 2 (Insight Level 3+, Normality must be below 60%):**
| Skill | EP Cost | Effect | How Steve Learned It |
|-------|---------|--------|---------------------|
| **Empathic Accuracy** | 5 EP | Read enemy's emotional state. Reveals which Talk actions will work. | Steve started actually listening to people. He doesn't know when this happened. |
| **The Nod** | 2 EP | A knowing, compassionate nod. De-escalates one enemy. +10% to next Talk action. | He's been in so many sharing circles that the nod is involuntary now. |
| **Somatic Release** | 6 EP | Cure all negative status effects on Steve. He shakes it off. Literally — trembling, like the breathwork people do. | His body started doing this on its own and he can't stop it. It works. He's furious. |

**Tier 3 (Insight Level 5+, Normality must be below 40%):**
| Skill | EP Cost | Effect | How Steve Learned It |
|-------|---------|--------|---------------------|
| **Hold Space** | 8 EP | Create a protective zone. All allies take 50% less damage for 2 turns. Enemies feel "held." | He doesn't know. He just... can. It's the thing he's most afraid of. |
| **Mirror Work** | 10 EP | Reflects all enemy attacks back for 1 turn. "I see you seeing me." | The Shadow Work Enthusiast wasn't entirely wrong. Steve has a shadow. It's an excellent accountant. |
| **Genuine Vulnerability** | 15 EP | Steve says something real. From the heart. No corporate filter. Massive psychic damage to all enemies + heals all allies. The most powerful ability in the game. Steve can only use it once per zone because it takes that much out of him. | The wife's journal entries. They cracked something open and he can't close it. |

## The Normality Meter (Visual Design)

The Normality meter should be visible on the HUD. It's a spectrum:

```
[CORPORATE] ========|====== [SPIRITUAL]
             Steve is here
```

- Starts at 90% (far corporate side)
- Equipment, items, and choices shift it
- The "sweet spot" for maximum gameplay options is around 50% — Steve speaks both languages but masters neither
- Going below 20% triggers special cutscenes where Steve catches himself using jargon unironically
- Going above 95% makes Steve so aggressively normal that spiritual enemies take damage just from being near him (funny but limits options)

## Companion System (If Implemented)

Later zones could introduce temporary companions who fight alongside Steve. Each represents a different relationship to the spiritual world:

| Companion | Zone Met | Specialty | Why They Help |
|-----------|----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Maya** (Skeptical Karma Yogi) | Zone 1 | Support/Intel — heals and reveals info | Steve helped her escape. She owes him. |
| **Frank** (Groundskeeper) | Zone 1 | Physical Tank — hits hard, takes hits | Likes Steve. Only normal person he's met in 12 years. |
| **The Disillusioned Facilitator** | Zone 3-4? | Spiritual DPS — powerful abilities, fragile ego | Left their retreat after Steve exposed something. Needs purpose. |
| **The Wife** (endgame?) | Final zone? | Hybrid — bridges both worlds | If the ending allows it. The most powerful companion because she genuinely bridges Steve's world and the spiritual one. |

Companions have their own skill sets and dialogue during combat. Frank just swings a wrench and says practical things. Maya provides sarcastic commentary and healing.
